Chance & Choice - virtual world building

Studio A : Chance | Studio B: Choice | Virtual World
  • We will be using a platform call Mozilla Hubs to build our Virtual worlds.
  • The following is a step by step guide to creating your world from your “chance and choice” zines.

1. Getting to know Hubs

Have a look over the intro to hubs documentation:

  • Go to hubs documentation URL and look at the following page:
    https://hubs.mozilla.com/docs/intro-hubs.html
  • or if you prefer video format, check out this video tutorial made by the bubs team: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5QnOsyyebEQ

Jump in and explore a hubs world

(You are not building your world yet, just getting a feel for the interface)

  • go to https://hubs.mozilla.com/
  • sign into hubs at this point (top right corner)

    • If your UTS email does not work, you will need to try to create your account with another email address

  • click create room (big pink button)
  • let the page load
  • click enter room
  • choose an avatar browse avatars then Accept
  • click Enter Now
  • (You may now be asked to accept the microphone permissions)
  • Get used to the controls in hubs
  • Start the interactive tour to learn how to navigate the interface (1 then 2 in the screenshot)
  • Check out a few of the pre-built scenes (1 then 3 in the screenshot)

2. Getting to know Spoke

We use the world building tool called spoke to build our virtual world

  • go to https://hubs.mozilla.com/spoke/projects/tutorial and work through the world building tutorial. This will give you the basics of working in Spoke to build a small virtual world.
  • Spend a little bit of time exploring the tool and also the assets available to you. in particular the architecture kit the rock kit, google poly and the hubs Sound Pack
  • For the moment you are just getting a feel for the material qualities of working in a 3d virtual environment.
  • You can also drag images in, which can become elements of your world. This is good if you are wanting to add text (.png file with text on a transparent background for example)

3. Build your world

now comes the time to really think about your virtual environment and how you will build it from the world you created for your zine

please ensure you log into hubs before proceeding, this will ensure you don’t lose your work. Your login will work for both Hubs and Spoke.

You need to think about your world in three dimensions, your audience will be experiencing it from a “first-person” perspective, so they will never be able to see it all at once as you can in the Spoke editor.

Think about the journey you take them on, and how you can reveal aspects of the world to them as they moved through it.

Now very quickly run through the following steps. Remember small, self contained worlds, with a logic you derive from your zine world is what we are after here…

  1. make a little sketched map of the world you want to create. Draw this from top down, or if you are feeling really inspired in isometric projection. See: https://duckduckgo.com/?q=isometric+map+drawing&atb=v235-1&iax=images&ia=images for inspiration.
  2. Draw a line(s) on the map to represent your user journey through the small world. What elements will you introduce to invite/force the user to take this journey?
  3. Open https://hubs.mozilla.com/spoke/ and create a new project.
  4. Use the architecture kit the rock kit, google poly and the hubs Sound Pack to build your world.
  5. SAVE OFTEN and test it in hubs by following the following procedure:
    1. click publish to hubs (top right of window)
    2. name your project (only if this is the first time you are doing this)
    3. click save project button
    4. click save and publish button
    5. click publish scene button (might take a few moments to upload)
    6. click view your scene button
    7. click create a room with this scene button and open your scene in hubs.
  6. Continue to iterate your world until you are ready to share it.
  7. Rememeber sound is "spatial" in hubs, use a little bit wisely in your world. See Freesound, if you want to find more samples for your project

4. share your world

  1. You need to open your world as a room in Hubs to share it.
  2. Once you share it, take note of the URL for your new room. This is a unique URL and if several people go to it, you will all be in the one virtual environment.
  3. You also need to include this in your final website and folio.

5. Resources & Inspiration

Luminous Rock World

This is a small world Andrew made, based on the "Luminous Rock World" demo zine from the world building/zine workshop. It only uses a few assets - rocks from rock kit and clouds from poly, plus a red light and 3 audio files i imported. You can open it in Spoke and check out how it's made and also look at it in Hubs as a room. Also note the "sky box" has been changed to make the sky darker. Use lights sparingly as these take a bit of computer power to render. Lights can be found in the "elements" section of the assets

This is a link to the scene https://hubs.mozilla.com/scenes/q2iayuj

This is a sped up screen cast of the first part of making the Luminous Rock World in Spoke. This only shows part of the design journey, but you will see from the video that this is an iterative design process, and that it takes several iterations to get on the right path, that will eventually lead to the outcome shown above.

Desiderium

Hayley Zheng's Desiderium is a wonderful example of a series of complex virtual worlds created using Poly assets. It is created in the Unity game engine and has a complex underlying narrative and an interactive user journey--Something to aspire to in your future endevours designing virtual environments.
